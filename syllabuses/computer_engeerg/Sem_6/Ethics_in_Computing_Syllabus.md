### Course Description:
This course explores the ethical and social implications of computing technologies. Students will examine a range of ethical dilemmas that arise in the field of computer science, including issues related to privacy, security, intellectual property, and professional responsibility. Through case studies, discussions, and written assignments, students will develop critical thinking skills and learn to apply ethical frameworks to complex computing scenarios. The course aims to equip students with the knowledge and skills necessary to make informed and ethical decisions throughout their careers as computing professionals.

### Prerequisites:
None

### Learning Objectives:
1.  Analyze ethical dilemmas that arise in the development and use of computing technologies.
2.  Apply ethical frameworks, such as utilitarianism and deontology, to evaluate computing-related issues.
3.  Assess the social and legal implications of computing technologies, including privacy, security, and intellectual property.
4.  Formulate and defend ethical positions on controversial issues in computing.
5.  Demonstrate an understanding of professional codes of ethics for computing professionals.
6.  Evaluate the impact of computing on society, including issues of bias, accessibility, and environmental sustainability.

### Required Textbooks:
