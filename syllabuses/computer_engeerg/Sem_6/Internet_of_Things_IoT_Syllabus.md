### Course Description:
This course provides a comprehensive introduction to the Internet of Things (IoT). Students will learn about the fundamental concepts, architectures, protocols, and technologies that enable the development and deployment of IoT systems. The course covers various aspects of IoT, including sensing, data acquisition, communication, data processing, and application development. Students will gain hands-on experience through programming assignments and a final IoT project.

### Prerequisites:
Introduction to Computer Networks, Data Structures and Algorithms

### Learning Objectives:
1.  Describe the fundamental concepts and building blocks of the Internet of Things.
2.  Analyze different IoT architectures and communication protocols.
3.  Implement IoT solutions using various hardware and software platforms.
4.  Evaluate the security and privacy challenges in IoT systems.
5.  Design and develop IoT applications for different domains.
6.  Apply data analytics techniques to extract insights from IoT data.

### Required Textbooks:
- David Hanes, Gonzalo Salgueiro, Patrick Grossetete, Rob Barton, Jerome Henry, *IoT Fundamentals: Networking Technologies, Protocols, and Use Cases for the Internet of Things*, Cisco Press, 2017 (ISBN: 978-1-58714-456-1)

### Recommended Readings:
- Article: "A Survey on Security and Privacy Issues in Internet of Things", *IEEE Internet of Things Journal*, Volume 5(2):854-863, 2018.

### Weekly Schedule:
**Week 1: Introduction to IoT**
- Topics: What is IoT? History and evolution of IoT, IoT ecosystem, IoT characteristics, IoT applications, Societal impact of IoT.
- Readings: Chapter 1 of the textbook.
- Activities: Introduction to the course, discussion on IoT applications.
- Due: N/A

**Week 2: IoT Architectures**
- Topics: IoT reference architecture, Device layer, Network layer, Data management layer, Application layer.
- Readings: Chapter 2 of the textbook.
- Activities: Case study on different IoT architectures.
- Due: N/A

**Week 3: IoT Communication Protocols - Part 1**
- Topics: Wireless communication fundamentals, IEEE 802.15.4, Zigbee, Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE).
- Readings: Chapters 3 & 4 of the textbook.
- Activities: Lab session: Introduction to wireless communication modules.
- Due: N/A

**Week 4: IoT Communication Protocols - Part 2**
- Topics: Wi-Fi, Cellular technologies (LTE, 5G), LoRaWAN, MQTT, CoAP.
- Readings: Chapters 5 & 6 of the textbook.
- Activities: Comparison of different IoT communication protocols.
- Due: Programming Assignment 1: Implementing MQTT client.

**Week 5: IoT Hardware Platforms - Part 1**
- Topics: Introduction to embedded systems, Microcontrollers (Arduino, ESP32), Sensors and actuators.
- Readings: Online resources on Arduino and ESP32.
- Activities: Lab session: Working with Arduino and basic sensors.

**Week 6: IoT Hardware Platforms - Part 2**

- Readings: Selected research papers on IoT data management.
- Activities: Discussion on data acquisition challenges.

**Week 8: IoT Cloud Platforms**
