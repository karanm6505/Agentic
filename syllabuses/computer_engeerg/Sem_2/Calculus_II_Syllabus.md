### Course Description:
This course is the second semester of single-variable calculus, focusing on advanced techniques of integration, applications of integration, infinite sequences and series, and an introduction to multivariable calculus. Students will develop a strong understanding of these concepts through rigorous problem-solving and theoretical exploration. The course emphasizes both computational skills and conceptual understanding, preparing students for further study in mathematics, science, and engineering.

### Prerequisites:
Calculus I (MATH 201) or equivalent

### Learning Objectives:
1.  Master advanced integration techniques, including trigonometric substitution, partial fractions, and improper integrals.
2.  Apply integration to solve problems involving areas, volumes, arc length, and surface area.
3.  Analyze and determine the convergence or divergence of infinite sequences and series using various tests.
4.  Represent functions as power series and utilize power series for approximation and problem-solving.
5.  Perform basic operations on vectors and understand their geometric interpretation.
6.  Compute partial derivatives and apply them to optimization problems in multivariable calculus.

### Required Textbooks:

### Recommended Readings:
- Thomas Finney, *Calculus and Analytic Geometry*, Addison-Wesley, 2017.

### Weekly Schedule:
**Week 1: Review of Integration**
- Topics: Basic integration formulas, substitution rule, integration by parts.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 5
- Activities: Review problems, Q&A session.
- Due: N/A

**Week 2: Trigonometric Integrals and Trigonometric Substitution**
- Topics: Integrals involving trigonometric functions, trigonometric substitution.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 7.2, 7.3
- Activities: Problem-solving workshop.
- Due: N/A

**Week 3: Integration by Partial Fractions**
- Topics: Partial fraction decomposition, integrating rational functions.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 7.4
- Activities: Group work on partial fractions.
- Due: Problem Set 1 (Review of Integration, Trig Integrals)

**Week 4: Improper Integrals**
- Topics: Improper integrals of type 1 and type 2, convergence and divergence.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 7.8
- Activities: Discussion on applications of improper integrals.
- Due: N/A

**Week 5: Applications of Integration: Area and Volume**
- Topics: Area between curves, volumes by slicing, volumes of revolution.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 6.1, 6.2
- Activities: Visualization of volumes using software.
- Due: Quiz 1 (Trigonometric Substitution, Partial Fractions)

**Week 6: Applications of Integration: Arc Length and Surface Area**
- Topics: Arc length of a curve, surface area of a solid of revolution.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 6.3, 6.4
- Activities: Derivation of arc length formula.
- Due: N/A

**Week 7: Sequences**
- Topics: Definition of a sequence, limit of a sequence, convergence and divergence.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 11.1
- Activities: Examples of convergent and divergent sequences.
- Due: N/A

**Week 8: Series**
- Topics: Definition of a series, partial sums, convergence and divergence.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 11.2
- Activities: Discussion on the harmonic series.
- Due: N/A

**Week 9: The Integral Test and Comparison Tests**
- Topics: Integral test, comparison test, limit comparison test.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 11.3, 11.4
- Activities: Application of tests to determine convergence.
- Due: Exam 1 (Integration Techniques and Applications)

**Week 10: Alternating Series and Absolute Convergence**
- Topics: Alternating series test, absolute and conditional convergence, ratio test, root test.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 11.5, 11.6
- Activities: Examples of conditionally convergent series.
- Due: N/A

**Week 11: Power Series**
- Topics: Definition of a power series, radius and interval of convergence.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 11.8
- Activities: Finding the interval of convergence of power series.
- Due: N/A

**Week 12: Representations of Functions as Power Series**
- Topics: Representing functions as power series, Taylor and Maclaurin series.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 11.9, 11.10
- Activities: Deriving Taylor series for common functions.
- Due: Problem Set 3 (Convergence Tests, Power Series)

**Week 13: Vectors and the Geometry of Space**
- Topics: Three-dimensional coordinate system, vectors, dot product, cross product.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 12.1, 12.2, 12.3, 12.4
- Activities: Vector operations and geometric interpretations.
- Due: N/A

**Week 14: Vector Functions and Space Curves**
- Topics: Vector-valued functions, derivatives and integrals of vector functions, arc length and curvature.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 13.1, 13.2, 13.3
- Activities: Parametric equations and space curves.
- Due: N/A

**Week 15: Partial Derivatives**
- Topics: Functions of several variables, partial derivatives, tangent planes.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 14.1, 14.3, 14.4
- Activities: Computing partial derivatives and visualizing tangent planes.
- Due: Quiz 2 (Sequences and Series)

**Week 16: Optimization**
- Topics: Maximum and minimum values, Lagrange multipliers.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 14.7, 14.8
- Activities: Solving optimization problems using Lagrange multipliers.
- Due: Exam 2 (Series, Vectors, and Multivariable Calculus)
