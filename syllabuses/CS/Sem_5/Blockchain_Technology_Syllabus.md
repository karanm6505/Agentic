### Course Description:
This course provides a comprehensive introduction to blockchain technology, exploring its fundamental concepts, underlying principles, and practical applications. Students will gain a solid understanding of cryptography, consensus mechanisms, and distributed ledger technology. The course will also delve into the specifics of Bitcoin and Ethereum, smart contract development, and the potential of blockchain across various industries. Students will learn to critically evaluate the benefits and limitations of blockchain technology, as well as its impact on the future of information systems.

### Prerequisites:
Introduction to Computer Science, Data Structures and Algorithms

### Learning Objectives:
1.  Define and explain the core concepts of blockchain technology, including decentralization, immutability, and transparency.
2.  Analyze the cryptographic principles that underpin blockchain systems, such as hashing, digital signatures, and Merkle trees.
3.  Evaluate different consensus mechanisms (e.g., Proof-of-Work, Proof-of-Stake) and their implications for blockchain performance and security.
4.  Implement basic smart contracts using Solidity and deploy them on a test network.
5.  Assess the architecture and functionality of Bitcoin and Ethereum, including their respective strengths and weaknesses.
6.  Examine real-world applications of blockchain technology in areas such as finance, supply chain management, and healthcare.
7.  Critically evaluate the legal, ethical, and societal implications of blockchain technology.

### Required Textbooks:

- Imran Bashir, *Mastering Blockchain*, Packt Publishing, 2017 (ISBN: 978-1787125371)

### Recommended Readings:
- Buterin, V. "Ethereum White Paper." Ethereum Foundation, 2014.

### Weekly Schedule:
**Week 1: Introduction to Blockchain Technology**
- Topics: What is Blockchain?, History, Decentralization, Immutability, Transparency, Types of Blockchains.
- Readings: Bashir, Chapter 1
- Activities: Discussion: What problems does blockchain solve?
- Due: N/A

**Week 2: Cryptography Basics**
- Topics: Hashing, Digital Signatures, Public Key Cryptography, Private Key Management.
- Readings: Antonopoulos, Chapter 3
- Activities: Lab: Implementing basic cryptographic functions in Python.
- Due: N/A

**Week 3: Blockchain Architecture**
- Topics: Blocks, Transactions, Merkle Trees, Block Headers, Blockchain Data Structures.
- Readings: Bashir, Chapter 2
- Activities: Discussion: Analyzing different block header components.
- Due: N/A

**Week 4: Consensus Mechanisms: Proof-of-Work**
- Topics: Mining, Difficulty Adjustment, 51% Attack, Energy Consumption.
- Readings: Antonopoulos, Chapter 4
- Activities: Lab: Simulating a Proof-of-Work mining process.

**Week 6: Bitcoin: Introduction and Architecture**

- Readings: Antonopoulos, Chapter 5

- Due: N/A
