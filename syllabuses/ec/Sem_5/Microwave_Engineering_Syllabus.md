# Course Syllabus
## Course Title: Microwave Engineering
**Course Code:** EE330
**Credits:** 3
**Semester:** Fall 2024
**Instructor:** Dr. Eleanor Vance
**Contact:** eleanor.vance@university.edu
**Office Hours:** Tuesdays and Thursdays 2:00 PM - 3:30 PM or By Appointment

### Course Description:
This course provides a comprehensive introduction to the principles and applications of microwave engineering. Students will gain a fundamental understanding of microwave components, transmission line theory, and waveguide theory. The course will cover the analysis and design of microwave circuits, including impedance matching, filter design, and amplifier design. Laboratory experiments will complement the theoretical concepts, providing hands-on experience with microwave measurement techniques and circuit implementation.

### Prerequisites:
Electromagnetics I, Circuit Analysis

### Learning Objectives:
1.  Analyze the behavior of electromagnetic waves in microwave circuits and components.
2.  Apply transmission line theory to solve impedance matching problems and analyze signal propagation.
3.  Evaluate the characteristics and applications of different types of waveguides.
4.  Design and simulate basic microwave circuits, including filters and matching networks.
5.  Implement microwave circuits in a laboratory setting, using appropriate test equipment.
6.  Differentiate between various microwave components and their applications.
7.  Demonstrate proficiency in using the Smith chart for microwave circuit design.

### Required Textbooks:
- David M. Pozar, *Microwave Engineering*, 4th Edition, Wiley, 2011 (ISBN: 978-0470631553)

### Recommended Readings:
- Thomas H. Lee, *Planar Microwave Engineering*, Cambridge University Press, 2004.
- Article Title: "Microwave Filters for Communication Systems," *IEEE Transactions on Microwave Theory and Techniques*, Vol. 50, No. 1, 2002.

### Weekly Schedule:
**Week 1: Introduction to Microwaves**
- Topics: Microwave spectrum, applications, advantages, and disadvantages of microwaves; review of electromagnetic fundamentals.
- Readings: Pozar, Chapter 1
- Activities: Introduction to the course, syllabus review, discussion of microwave applications.
- Due: N/A

**Week 2: Transmission Line Theory I**
- Topics: Transmission line equations, characteristic impedance, reflection coefficient, and standing wave ratio.
- Readings: Pozar, Chapter 2
- Activities: Problem-solving session on transmission line parameters.
- Due: Homework 1

**Week 3: Transmission Line Theory II**
- Topics: Impedance matching techniques, single-stub matching, and double-stub matching.
- Readings: Pozar, Chapter 2
- Activities: Lab Session 1: Transmission Line Measurements (VSWR, Impedance).
- Due: Lab Report 1

**Week 4: The Smith Chart I**
- Topics: Introduction to the Smith chart, plotting impedance and admittance, and using the Smith chart for impedance matching.
- Readings: Pozar, Chapter 2
- Activities: Smith chart practice problems.
- Due: Homework 2

**Week 5: The Smith Chart II**
- Topics: Smith chart applications for impedance matching.
- Readings: Pozar, Chapter 2
- Activities: Smith chart exercises, design examples.
- Due: Quiz 1

**Week 6: Waveguides I**
- Topics: Rectangular waveguides, TE and TM modes, cutoff frequency, and wave impedance.
- Readings: Pozar, Chapter 3
- Activities: Discussion of waveguide modes and propagation.
- Due: Homework 3

**Week 7: Waveguides II**
- Topics: Circular waveguides, waveguide losses, and waveguide discontinuities.
- Readings: Pozar, Chapter 3
- Activities: Lab Session 2: Waveguide Measurements.
- Due: Lab Report 2

**Week 8: Midterm Exam**
- Topics: Covers all material from Weeks 1-7.
- Readings: Review all textbook chapters and lecture notes.
- Activities: Exam.
- Due: N/A

**Week 9: Microwave Components I**
- Topics: Directional couplers, power dividers, and hybrid junctions.
- Readings: Pozar, Chapter 4
- Activities: Discussion of directional coupler applications.
- Due: Homework 4

**Week 10: Microwave Components II**
- Topics: Filters: Filter design principles, low-pass, high-pass, band-pass, and band-stop filters.
- Readings: Pozar, Chapter 4
- Activities: Filter design examples.
- Due: N/A

**Week 11: Microwave Filters**
- Topics: Filter design techniques: insertion loss method, filter implementation.
- Readings: Pozar, Chapter 4
- Activities: Lab Session 3: Filter Design and Measurement.
- Due: Lab Report 3

**Week 12: Microwave Amplifiers**
- Topics: Microwave transistor characteristics, amplifier design principles, and stability analysis.
- Readings: Pozar, Chapter 5
- Activities: Amplifier design examples.
- Due: Homework 5

**Week 13: Microwave Amplifier Design**
- Topics: Amplifier design, matching networks, and stability considerations.
- Readings: Pozar, Chapter 5
- Activities: Amplifier design simulation.
- Due: N/A

**Week 14: Microwave Measurements**
- Topics: Network analyzers, spectrum analyzers, and power meters.
- Readings: Pozar, Chapter 6
- Activities: Lab Session 4: Network Analyzer Measurements.
- Due: Lab Report 4

**Week 15: Microwave Circuit Design Project Presentations**
- Topics: Student project presentations.
- Readings: Review all course material.
- Activities: Student presentations and peer review.
- Due: Project Presentations

**Week 16: Final Exam**
- Topics: Comprehensive exam covering all course materials.
- Readings: Review all textbook chapters and lecture notes.
- Activities: Exam.
- Due: N/A
