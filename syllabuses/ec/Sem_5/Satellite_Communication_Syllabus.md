# Course Syllabus
## Course Title: Satellite Communication
**Course Code:** EE345
**Credits:** 3
**Semester:** Fall 2024
**Instructor:** Dr. Anya Sharma
**Contact:** anya.sharma@university.edu
**Office Hours:** Tuesdays and Thursdays 2:00 PM - 3:00 PM or By Appointment

### Course Description:
This course provides a comprehensive introduction to the principles and applications of satellite communication systems. Students will explore the fundamental concepts of orbital mechanics, including satellite orbits, launch vehicles, and satellite subsystems. The course will delve into the design and analysis of satellite communication links, covering topics such as link budget calculations, modulation techniques, multiple access methods, and the impact of atmospheric effects. Practical aspects of satellite communication, including earth station technology and satellite services, will also be examined.

### Prerequisites:
Linear Systems, Signals and Systems, Probability and Random Processes

### Learning Objectives:
1.  Analyze satellite orbits and their characteristics, including Keplerian elements and perturbations.
2.  Evaluate the performance of satellite communication links using link budget calculations.
3.  Implement various modulation and multiple access techniques for satellite communication systems.
4.  Design and assess satellite communication systems considering factors such as interference and propagation effects.
5.  Compare and contrast different satellite services and applications, including broadcasting, mobile communications, and remote sensing.
6.  Apply knowledge to solve practical problems related to satellite communication system design and operation.
7.  Evaluate the impact of the space environment on satellite systems.

### Required Textbooks:
-   Prat, F., *Satellite Communications*, Wiley, 2016 (ISBN: 978-1118937714)

### Recommended Readings:
-   Maral, G., Bousquet, M., *Satellite Communications Systems: Systems, Implementation and Applications*, Wiley, 2009.
-   "The ITU and Satellite Communications," *ITU News*, 2018.

### Weekly Schedule:
**Week 1: Introduction to Satellite Communication**
-   Topics: Overview of satellite communication systems, historical perspective, applications, and future trends.
-   Readings: Prat, Chapter 1
-   Activities: Course introduction, discussion of course objectives and expectations.
-   Due: N/A

**Week 2: Orbital Mechanics I**
-   Topics: Kepler's Laws, orbital parameters, coordinate systems, and satellite orbits.
-   Readings: Prat, Chapter 2
-   Activities: Problem-solving session on orbital elements.
-   Due: Homework 1

**Week 3: Orbital Mechanics II**
-   Topics: Orbit perturbations, launch vehicles, and orbital maneuvers.
-   Readings: Prat, Chapter 2
-   Activities: Lab session: Orbital simulation using software.
-   Due: N/A

**Week 4: Satellite Subsystems**
-   Topics: Satellite bus, power systems, attitude control, and telemetry, tracking, and command (TT&C).
-   Readings: Prat, Chapter 3
-   Activities: Discussion on satellite design considerations.
-   Due: Quiz 1

**Week 5: Radio Wave Propagation**
-   Topics: Electromagnetic wave propagation, atmospheric effects (rain fade, scintillation), and free space loss.
-   Readings: Prat, Chapter 4
-   Activities: Problem-solving session on propagation losses.
-   Due: Homework 2

**Week 6: Link Budget Analysis I**
-   Topics: Introduction to link budget, Friis transmission equation, and antenna gain.
-   Readings: Prat, Chapter 5
-   Activities: Link budget calculations examples.
-   Due: N/A

**Week 7: Link Budget Analysis II**
-   Topics: Noise temperature, C/N ratio, and link margin calculations.
-   Readings: Prat, Chapter 5
-   Activities: Case study: Link budget analysis for a specific satellite system.
-   Due: Project Proposal

**Week 8: Modulation Techniques**
-   Topics: Review of modulation techniques (BPSK, QPSK, etc.), bandwidth efficiency, and power efficiency.
-   Readings: Prat, Chapter 6
-   Activities: Discussion on modulation for satellite communication.
-   Due: N/A

**Week 9: Midterm Exam**
-   Topics: All topics covered in Weeks 1-8
-   Readings: N/A
-   Activities: Exam
-   Due: Midterm Exam

**Week 10: Multiple Access Techniques I**
-   Topics: FDMA, TDMA, and CDMA.
-   Readings: Prat, Chapter 7
-   Activities: Discussion on multiple access techniques in satellite communications.
-   Due: Homework 3

**Week 11: Multiple Access Techniques II**
-   Topics: Comparison of multiple access techniques, efficiency and capacity.
-   Readings: Prat, Chapter 7
-   Activities: Problem solving session.
-   Due: N/A

**Week 12: Earth Station Technology**
-   Topics: Antennas, RF equipment, and ground segment considerations.
-   Readings: Prat, Chapter 8
-   Activities: Guest speaker: Industry professional in satellite communication.
-   Due: Quiz 2

**Week 13: Satellite Services**
-   Topics: Broadcasting, mobile satellite services, and internet via satellite.
-   Readings: Prat, Chapter 9
-   Activities: Case study: Analysis of a specific satellite service.
-   Due: N/A

**Week 14: Interference and Mitigation**
-   Topics: Interference sources, types of interference, and mitigation techniques.
-   Readings: Prat, Chapter 10
-   Activities: Discussion on interference management.
-   Due: Homework 4

**Week 15: Advanced Topics and Future Trends**
-   Topics: New satellite technologies, including small satellites, and high-throughput satellites.
-   Readings: Selected research papers
-   Activities: Student presentations on selected topics.
-   Due: Project Report Draft

**Week 16: Project Presentations and Final Exam Review**
-   Topics: Project presentations and course review.
-   Readings: N/A
-   Activities: Project presentations, Q&A, and exam preparation.
-   Due: Final Project Report
