### Course Description:
This course provides a comprehensive introduction to the principles and applications of cognitive radio technology. Students will explore the fundamental concepts of dynamic spectrum access, spectrum sensing, and radio resource management. The course will cover various aspects of cognitive radio systems, including spectrum policy, implementation challenges, and emerging research trends. Through lectures, simulations, and projects, students will gain a solid understanding of cognitive radio design and its role in future wireless communication systems.

### Prerequisites:
Signals and Systems, Probability and Random Processes, Wireless Communications Fundamentals

### Learning Objectives:
1.  Define and explain the key concepts of cognitive radio and its evolution.
2.  Analyze different spectrum sensing techniques and their performance trade-offs.
3.  Implement dynamic spectrum access algorithms for efficient spectrum utilization.
4.  Evaluate various radio resource management strategies in cognitive radio networks.
5.  Design and simulate cognitive radio systems using industry-standard tools.
6.  Compare and contrast different cognitive radio architectures and their applications.
7.  Critically assess the impact of cognitive radio on wireless communication and regulatory policies.

### Required Textbooks:
-   Joseph Mitola III, *Cognitive Radio: An Integrated Agent Architecture*, John Wiley & Sons, 2000 (ISBN: 978-0471384890)
-   Bruce Fette, *Cognitive Radio Technology*, Newnes, 2006 (ISBN: 978-0750679436)

### Recommended Readings:

-   "Cognitive Radio for Dynamic Spectrum Access: Challenges and Solutions," *IEEE Wireless Communications*, Volume 13, Issue 2, pp. 16-23, 2006.
