### Course Description:
This course explores the transformative impact of artificial intelligence (AI) on the labor market and the future of work. Students will examine how AI technologies are reshaping industries, automating tasks, and creating new job roles. The course will delve into the ethical, social, and economic implications of AI-driven automation, focusing on the skills and knowledge needed to thrive in an increasingly AI-integrated workplace. Students will develop critical thinking skills to analyze the challenges and opportunities presented by AI, preparing them to navigate the evolving landscape of work.

### Prerequisites:
ECON 101, STAT 200, or equivalent

### Learning Objectives:
1.  Analyze the current and projected impact of AI on various industries and job categories.
2.  Evaluate the ethical considerations surrounding AI-driven automation and its effects on employment.
3.  Identify the emerging skills and competencies required for success in the future of work.
4.  Assess the potential for AI to augment human capabilities and enhance productivity.
5.  Develop strategies for adapting to the changing demands of the labor market in the age of AI.
6.  Communicate effectively about the complex issues surrounding AI and the future of work.

### Required Textbooks:
- Frey, Carl Benedikt, *The Technology Trap: Capital, Labor, and Power in the Age of Automation*, Princeton University Press, 2019 (ISBN: 9780691172795)

### Recommended Readings:
- Brynjolfsson, E., & McAfee, A. (2014). *The second machine age: Work, progress, and prosperity in a time of brilliant technologies*. WW Norton & Company.
- Autor, D. H. (2015). Why are there still so many jobs? The history and future of workplace automation. *Journal of Economic Perspectives*, *29*(3), 3-30.
