### Course Description:
This course provides an introduction to single-variable calculus. Topics include limits, continuity, derivatives of algebraic and trigonometric functions, applications of derivatives such as optimization and related rates, and an introduction to integration. The course emphasizes conceptual understanding, computational skills, and problem-solving techniques.

### Prerequisites:
Precalculus or equivalent

### Learning Objectives:
1.  Compute limits and derivatives of algebraic and trigonometric functions.
2.  Apply the concept of the derivative to solve optimization and related rates problems.
3.  Evaluate definite and indefinite integrals using basic integration techniques.
4.  Understand and apply the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus.
5.  Model real-world phenomena using calculus concepts.
6.  Develop problem-solving skills in calculus through application and computation.

### Required Textbooks:
- James Stewart, *Calculus: Early Transcendentals*, 9th Edition, Cengage Learning, 2020 (ISBN: 978-1337776340)

### Recommended Readings:
- Thomas Finney, *Calculus and Analytic Geometry*, Addison-Wesley, 1995.
- Article Title: "The Historical Development of Calculus", Mathematics Magazine, 62(3):163-180, 1989.

### Weekly Schedule:
**Week 1: Introduction to Calculus and Limits**
- Topics: Functions, graphs, and limits. Techniques for evaluating limits.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 1 & 2.1-2.3
- Activities: Introduction to the course, review of precalculus concepts.
- Due: N/A

**Week 2: Continuity**
- Topics: Definition of continuity, types of discontinuities, Intermediate Value Theorem.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 2.4-2.6
- Activities: Problem-solving session on limits and continuity.
- Due: N/A

**Week 3: The Derivative**
- Topics: Definition of the derivative, tangent lines, rates of change.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 3.1-3.3
- Activities: Introduction to differentiation rules.
- Due: Problem Set 1

**Week 4: Differentiation Rules**
- Topics: Power rule, product rule, quotient rule, derivatives of trigonometric functions.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 3.4-3.6
- Activities: Practice differentiating various functions.
- Due: N/A

**Week 5: The Chain Rule**
- Topics: Chain rule, implicit differentiation.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 3.7-3.9
- Activities: Applying the chain rule to complex functions.
- Due: Quiz 1

**Week 6: Related Rates**
- Topics: Solving related rates problems.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 4.1
- Activities: Group work on related rates problems.
- Due: N/A

**Week 7: Maximum and Minimum Values**
- Topics: Finding maximum and minimum values of functions, critical points.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 4.2
- Activities: Optimization problems.
- Due: Problem Set 2

**Week 8: The Mean Value Theorem**
- Topics: The Mean Value Theorem, increasing and decreasing functions.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 4.3
- Activities: Applications of the Mean Value Theorem.
- Due: N/A

**Week 9: First Exam**
- Topics: Chapters 1-4.3
- Readings: Review Chapters 1-4.3
- Activities: Review session
- Due: Exam 1

**Week 10: Curve Sketching**
- Topics: Using derivatives to sketch curves, concavity, inflection points.
- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 4.4
- Activities: Sketching curves using calculus techniques.
- Due: N/A

**Week 11: Optimization Problems**

- Readings: Stewart, Chapter 4.5

**Week 12: Introduction to Integration**
